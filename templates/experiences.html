Responsible A.I.

In the 2023-2024 academic year, Amanda participated in the University of Utah Honors Praxis Lab, a multidisciplinary cohort focused on examining the development, application, and societal impact of artificial intelligence. Through collaborative research and discussion, she explored both the technical and ethical dimensions of modern AI systems.

  • Audited the algorithm behind Spotify's AI-generated “Daylist” feature.
  • Developed public-facing resources to support AI literacy and accessibility.
  • Presented research findings at the end-of-year symposium.

This experience strengthened Amanda's understanding of responsible AI development and reinforced her interest in building technology that is both effective and socially conscious.

Drum Corps International

Drum Corps International, often referred to as “Marching Music's Major League,” provides a highly competitive performance environment where musicians train intensively throughout the summer season. Members commit to long rehearsal days focused on precision, endurance, and ensemble excellence.

Amanda served as a mellophone soloist and section leader with The Battalion Drum & Bugle Corps in 2023, balancing both performance and leadership responsibilities.

  • Independently prepared and refined solo performances.
  • Collaborated with ensemble members to maintain consistent performance quality.
  • Mentored younger members of the section.
  • Applied and incorporated feedback from instructional staff.

This role challenged Amanda to develop quickly as both a musician and leader, strengthening her discipline, adaptability, and commitment to high-performance environments.

High Point Camp

During the summer of 2024, Amanda worked as an overnight counselor at High Point Camp, the same camp she attended growing up. The role involved supporting campers in a structured, collaborative environment designed to promote safety, fun, and spiritual growth.

During the week, she led activities and provided supervision and support for campers of varying ages and backgrounds. On weekends, staff prepared and maintained camp facilities to ensure a safe and welcoming environment.

  • Maintained safety protocols as a certified Red Cross lifeguard.
  • Supported campers in resolving interpersonal conflicts.
  • Encouraged emotional, social, and spiritual development in group settings.
  • Led activity stations while ensuring appropriate supervision.

This experience strengthened Amanda's leadership, responsibility, and ability to create supportive environments for others, while reinforcing the importance of teamwork and community engagement.

SRAS: Bishkek

In 2025, Amanda spent eight weeks studying abroad in Bishkek, Kyrgyzstan, where she lived with a host family and attended daily language classes in the city. The program also included cultural excursions that provided direct exposure to Kyrgyz history, traditions, and daily life.

During her time abroad, she developed:

  • Stronger communication skills through daily interaction in a new cultural and linguistic environment.
  • Greater cultural awareness and appreciation for diverse perspectives and ways of life.
  • Increased adaptability in unfamiliar and dynamic environments.
  • Improved problem-solving skills through real-world, independent experiences.

The experience reinforced Amanda’s commitment to stepping outside her comfort zone and embracing unfamiliar environments. Living abroad for the first time accelerated her personal growth and strengthened her adaptability and communication skills.
